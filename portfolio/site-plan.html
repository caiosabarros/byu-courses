Content

Home page

Welcome to my portfolio website! This website is designed for you, a recruiter! If you are not a recruiter, thanks for visiting anyways! The page shows my work in two different areas that I have adventured myself in: web3 and cybersecurity. In both you will find a mix of educational content I have created - or completed - as well as my work in the area, which may be different for each category. In the future, I intend to also add my work in quantum cybernetics. Actually, this is where I am trying to head towards with my work in web3 and cyber. Have a look at both pages and see what you like about it!

Images for the Home page

Web3

Since 2021, I have gained interest in the blockchain industry, mostly because of its mission delete the middle-man of the game in everything we do. In this page, you will get links to verified smart contracts I have deployed on different mainnets/testnets and also my work on open-source content, whether it be educational or functional. My experience varies from deploying more than 7 contracts on mainnet, writing about ten different articles to help in the education of the community and also creating high-quality videos for it. The main tools used in these articles and videos are: Remix, remixd, Geth, web3.js, testnets, Ganache, Truffle, Hardhat, mocha, etc. All these tools - as well as the more recent ones in the market, like Foundry - are a must for developing and testing smart contracts. Not only I helped in creating content for the community, but I also have been benefitted by it. I have participated in different bootcamps by Encode, like the Expert Solidity Bootcamp, ZK Bootcamp and soon

Soon, I will participate in the zkEVM Bootcamp. Therefore, this Web3 section also has some certificates I have received over the course of my education in web3, including the ones I have mentioned, but also other courses I have done by other courses providers, like the Smart Contract Hacking Course, that gave me enough experience to launch my current job: an independent smart contract security researcher. Hence, recently I have been using the knowledge acquired to search for vulnerabilities in live smart contracts that offer a bug bounty program.

My future ambitions in the Web3 space is to learn the ZK technology. This includes learning the Rust language, the L2's main funcitonalities and flows and search for vulnerabilities on these kind of protocols as well. I have a passion for development as well though, so alongside security I also intend to, in the future, develop something that adds value to the community, like a cool protocol. For example, since I have done some ML courses and I like it - not as web3, but I like it as well - I intend to do something related to the ZKML field. It is a new field with some research being done on it, but I think it is going to revolutionaze the power of AI when AI has privacy on it as well.

CyberSecurity

I began my experience in CyberSecurity in the year of 2023. It is recent therefore. But I found it fascinating because of how much I have to learn and how it requires something I love: creativity. You will see some certificates and CTFs I have completed in the field on this page.

My future ambitions in this area is to be able to learn all the attack vectors in a Rust-based approach. I intend then to translate the attack vectors I will learn in C or python into Rust, finish the course I am doing right now and complete some certifications in the field. After that, I want to dive into bug bounty programs as well, since these require lots of creativity, and I.LOVE.IT! A field of interest of mine for the future in CyberSecurity is quantum cybernetics. The idea of quantum computing and its possibility to break the RSA algorithms that have been used extensively in Web2 or Web3 is something that has been sparking lots of research so that we can try securing our systems in a post-quantum scenario.
